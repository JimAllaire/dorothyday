Explore the wisdom of Dorothy Day

"The greatest challenge of the day is: how to bring about a revolution of the heart, a revolution which has to start with each one of us."

Browse and search Dorothy Day's writings on peace, justice, pacifism, poverty, faith, nonviolence, conscience, work, the works of mercy, hospitality, community, the mystical body of Christ, the little way, and much more.

Overview

Users can download Dorothy Day's writings as individual reprints or for the purpose of creating other publications, digital or print.

For more information about Dorothy Day and the Catholic Worker Movement visit the companion website at The Catholic Worker Movement

Contents

  • A complete set of Dorothy Day's articles that appeared in The Catholic Worker newspaper from its beginnings in 1933 until her death in 1980. In all, 721 documents.
  • The complete text of four books: The Eleventh Virgin (1924), From Union Square to Rome (1938), House of Hospitality (1939), and On Pilgrimage (1948).
  • Selected articles from other publications.

Downloads

  • You can find articles to download by Browsing a list ordered by date, with titles and summaries
  • You can Search for single words or themes.
  • You may download articles in these file formats: PDF, DOC, HTML, and MD (markdown).
